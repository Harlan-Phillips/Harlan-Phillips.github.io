About me

I am currently studying Electrical Engineering and Computer Sciences at the University of California, Berkeley. With years of engineering experience, I excel in integration, full-stack development, cloud computing, data analysis, and machine learning.

In my current role at NASA Johnson Space Center, I work in the JSC Exploration Wireless Laboratory (JEWL) on an ambitious project to establish WiFi and cellular communication on the Moon. Specifically, we are developing lunar surface communication systems between spacesuits, the rover, and the lander. I am focusing on developing a WebRTC-based technology, the first of its kind designed for astronauts, to facilitate reliable and efficient communication on the lunar surface.

Simultaneously, I am conducting research at Cornell University, where I am developing a full-stack web application to classify astronomical transients using Flask, HTML, CSS, and SQL. I have deployed this application on Amazon AWS EC2 and am working on a machine learning model to automate and streamline classifications.

My professional journey includes impactful internships at NASA, where I contributed to cutting-edge projects at NASA Ames Research Center. Additionally, I gained valuable experience working at the Bureau of Land Management, focusing on infrared and magnetic sensors. Other relevant experience include my role as a Tech Fellow at Codepath and a mentor at Curious Cardinals.
